July 20, 2026 - Thoughts on the Avengers: Doomsday trailer

  Well, I've got a lot of things I want to talk about and some half-written posts, but life has been so crazy I haven't really been able to get everything down. It was a rough week last week. So that's on the way. Anyway, this morning, they dropped the first real trailer for Avengers: Doomsday. Gonna record my reaction after lunch and put it here. I'm writing this before I've seen it. After Thunderbolts*, I was nervous but cautiously optimistic about Doomsday. For the MCU, I care more about the overarching story of the MCU than the quality of any individual movie. If I just want to watch a movie, I can go see any other movie. I keep coming back to the MCU because of continuity. Regardless of quality, there still really isn't anything like the MCU in terms of telling stories across 40, 50 movies like this, so even if the movies suck sometimes, I can excuse it. Unfortunately, MCU leadership have shit the bed in terms of telling an overarching story the past two phases. It doesn't even feel like these movies take place in the same universe sometimes. Back in the Infinity Saga, you had Falcon in Winter Soldier, then he shows up in Ant-Man. You have Doctor Strange, then he shows up in Thor Ragnarok. It's more than just cameo bait, it rewarded you for keeping up with the MCU and made the world feel cohesive and connected. As for the Multiverse Saga, where has Shang-Chi been for the past 5 years? It just feels so aimless and decentralized. It's genuinely, insanely stupid that they didn't have a smaller- scale teamup/Avengers movie in the past few years. We got a little bit of that in The Marvels, which I think is better as a piece of the MCU than as a movie because it brings characters from so many different parts of the universe together. My favorite moment was when they had those Skrull refugees or something and Carol is like "Ok I know a guy" and then Valkyrie shows up on the Bifrost. That's the kind of connections I want to be seeing man! Like why has not a single soul tried to form an Avengers team until Julia Louis-Dreyfus? Not a SINGLE person even tried? Get off your ass Nick Fury! KAMALA KHAN tried to form a team before you did, queen that she is, even though, to my dismay, they'll probably be sidelined in this Doomsday bullshit. Anyway, the more I see from this movie the more I think it's going to be a shitshow. The MCU has been a shitshow for a while, but I told myself, "Yes, it's a mess, there's no connection, there's no cohesion, but if the Russos can pull it off and pull together every thread of the past 7 years, maybe I can walk away from this saga satisfied." I just don't think it's gonna happen. Especially with the addition of the X-Men, I'll get to that later. They're not going to care about the people like me who have continued to see their mid-ass movies through it all just for the love of it, they're going to throw everything away and pander to the people who have only seen Spider-Man, GOTG, and Deadpool. Moon Knight? Who's that? Kate Bishop? Stop talking crazy. We got the Fantastic Four and omg it's James Marsden! Seriously, the X-Men being here upsets me so much. The MCU is already a mess, but instead of trying to pull everything together, they're adding a bunch of characters that will take screentime from the characters that have ACTUALLY BEEN SET UP BY THE MOVIES AND TV SHOWS!!!!! I DON'T CARE!!! Deadpool and Wolverine was supposed to be saying goodbye to the Fox universe, so WHY ARE WE BRINGING THEM BACK!!!!!

  Ok, calming down. I'm going to go watch the trailer and report back. Maybe it will forever change my beliefs, or maybe it will confirm what I've known for a long time: the MCU is a dead man walking.
